Amy Eshleman is an American educator who advocates for children with disabilities. She was born in Cleveland, Ohio, USA. Eshleman began her career as a teacher and later advocated for children with disabilities, particularly those with Autism. She is known for her work in promoting inclusive education and for her role as the former First Lady of Chicago, where she used her platform to support various charitable organizations.

1980: Eshleman graduates from Kent State University with a degree in special education and begins her career as a teacher.

1994: Eshleman becomes the Director of Special Education for the Chicago Public Schools (CPS), where she works to promote inclusive education for children with disabilities.

2003: Eshleman becomes the Executive Director of the Chicago office of Autism Speaks, a national advocacy organization for individuals with Autism and their families.

2011: Eshleman becomes the First Lady of Chicago, as her husband, Rahm Emanuel, is elected Mayor. She uses her platform to support various charitable organizations, particularly those focused on education and children with disabilities.

2017: Eshleman retires from her role as Executive Director of Autism Speaks’ Chicago office but continues her advocacy work as a board member for various organizations, including the Special Olympics.

2020: Eshleman serves as a member of the COVID-19 Recovery Task Force for the city of Chicago, which aims to address the pandemic’s impact on vulnerable communities.
