Dorsa Derakhshani is an Iranian-American chess player. She was all over the news after she got banned from playing Chess without her hijab by her team. Who are her parents? Here is everything you want to know about her.

Dorsa rose to fame after winning three gold medals at Asian Youth Chess Championships in 2012, 2013, and 2014. She represented the Iranian team in the Women’s division of the Asian Nations Cup in 2012 and 2014.

However, she got banned from playing for the Iranian team in 2017, and she moved to the USA. Since then, she has been playing Chess for the U.S.

Dorsa Derakhshani Brother: Who Is He?

Dorsa Derakhshani’s brother, Borna Derakhshani, is also a chess player.

Previously, he represented Iran in Chess. But he also faced a ban from the Iranian Chess Federation after he played against Israeli grandmaster Alexander Huzban in the first round of the International Gibraltar Chess Tournament in January 2017.

Since then, he has been playing for England. After moving to England, he won the British Chess Youth Championship U16 category in 2018 and won in the U18 academy in 2019.

Dorsa Derakhshani Wikipedia Explored

According to the official Wikipedia page, Dorsa Derakhshani is an Iranian-American Chess player.

Dorsa received the titles Woman Grandmaster and International Master in 2016. In the same year, she also qualified for the title of FIDE trainer in 2016 and is an accredited FIDE journalist.

In 2017, she got banned by the Iranian Chess Federation for playing in the2017 Gibraltar Chess Tournament without wearing a hijab. Later, she moved to the USA and joined Saint Louis University to study Biology.

The former Iranian chess player got a license to play for the Unites States Chess Team following a ban. She competed in the 2018 U.S Women’s Chess Championship. She led her team to a bronze medal in the World Prestigious University Chess Invitational in Taiwan, China.
