Career & Progression

Josh grew up in rural North Carolina where he developed love for nature and adventure. He became a professional motocross and superbike racer in his 20s and won many titles with his fearless style.

He competed in different levels of bike racing, such as NCHSA and GNCC. He won the Heavy-C championship in NCHSA in 2003, and then 250-B in 2004. He moved up to 250-A in 2005, but a knee injury ended his racing career.

Josh still enjoys racing cars and bikes. In 2016, he joined the Southeast Gassers Association (SGA) and raced a 1953 Chevy Gasser at Brainerd Motorsports Park and Greer Dragway.

Josh also worked as a lumberjack and owns a lumberjack business, Tree and Yard Busters, in Polk County. He operates bobcats and dump trucks, climbs and cuts trees, and clears land.

Moonshining

Josh’s moonshining career began after he met Barney Barnwell, a legendary moonshiner from his local area. Barney taught Josh the art and science of making moonshine, a high-proof distilled spirit that is usually produced illegally. Josh was fascinated by the process and the history of moonshining and decided to follow in Barney’s footsteps.

Television

Josh accidentally became a reality television actor. It was his friend and mentor Barney Barnwell who was supposed to become a cast of Moonshiners. Josh was helping Barney to build an underground still for the project. After only two weeks of filming Barney died of a heart stroke. As the work was only at the initial phase camera crew and production team backed off.

Despite the uncertainty, Josh and his friend Bill Canny completed the work as a memorial for Barney which later turned out to be their shot at the television world. Discovery producers were impressed by the work and asked both of them to be cast in the docudrama, Moonshiners. Josh made his debut on the show in 2012 during season 2 episode 1 titled “Rise ‘n Shine!.” He has since become a regular cast member featuring in over 139 episodes until now.

Josh has gained a lot of fans and fame from his appearance on the show. He is admired for his no-nonsense attitude, outdoor abilities, and impressive liquor-making skills.

Accidents and Injuries

Josh Ownes’ passion for speed almost cost him his life when he was involved in a horrific crash at the Daytona International Speedway in March 2023. The accident happened during a bike race at the famous track in Daytona Beach, Florida. Owens was riding his motorcycle at high speed when he lost control and crashed into a wall. The impact was so severe that he broke his neck, back, arm, legs, and shoulder. He was rushed to the hospital with life-threatening injuries.  While fans were devasted by the news, rumors about his death started circulating on social media, but they have since been debunked. However, this is not the only time Josh has met with an accident and survived.  If the words from the man himself are anything to go by he is an unluckiest individual when it comes to accidents and injury. The man has gone on to say,

“I tend to be the guy that’s always at the wrong place at the right time…. or the right place at the wrong time.”

Looking at his past incidents, on March 16, 2018, Josh was hospitalized at Halifax Health Medical Center for injuries sustained in a motorcycle accident during Daytona Bike Week. He was thrown from an antique motorcycle while preparing for an exhibition race at the New Smyrna Speedway. According to the statement given by Josh, he had broken all but one of his ribs, punctured a lung, broken his collarbone and shoulder blade, and stripped the skin off one of his pointer fingers down to the bone.

On August 12, 2017, Josh witnessed his trailer attached to a truck burn after the fire broke out near Sloan Iowa on Interstate 29 Southbound at Mile Marker 130. He was backed off by Iowa police patrol as he tried to disconnect the truck from the trailer. Although he did not sustain any injury Josh lost the trailer to fire. The truck sustained damage to the tail lights, tailgate, and fenders, and a race motorcycle in the back of the truck also received some damage.
