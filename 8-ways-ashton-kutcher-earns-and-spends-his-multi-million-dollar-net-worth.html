Ashton Kutcher has come a long way since playing a pretty dumb boy on That 70s Show. He married the show’s co-star Mila Kunis and is a father to Wyatt, 7, and Dimitri, 4. The actor has starred in numerous hit movies and television shows over the years. While Kutcher has taken a step back from acting, he has amassed a $200 million fortune. It makes everyone curious to know what Ashton Kutcher has been up to and how he increased his net worth compared to his past co-stars. To pay for his lavish lifestyle, Kutcher has made numerous investments in different platforms.

Kutcher was way ahead of his time from start-ups to tech firms when he made quick investments in prospective ventures that have yielded him millions of dollars. He loves spending his money on trips with family and buying expensive automobiles. Let’s take a look at how Ashton Kutcher earns and spends his money.

Earns: Acting Career

Ashton Kutcher first gained popularity when he starred in the teen sitcom That 70s Show, where he portrayed Michael Kelso for seven seasons as a series regular. Kutcher made $250,000 to $300,000 per episode and earned $6 million to $8 million per season. Kutcher made millions in movies such as No Strings Attached, Jobs, and What Happens In Vegas. When he joined the already popular show Two And A Half Men as the lead, he earned $700,000 per episode for replacing the void which was left by Charlie Sheen’s departure, as noted by Screen Rant.

Spends: Stunning Car Collection

Ashton Kutcher is sported driving impressive cars on the street when he goes out to a restaurant or picks groceries with the kids. Kutcher is sometimes spotted cruising in his red Ferrari California in Hollywood. Ashton Kutcher went electric by buying a Tesla Model S in 2013. He is seen chiefly driving his family around in the car when they go out for a casual run to the city. On the more vintage side, he owns a bright pink 1996 Impala SS. The color and design of the car are said to reflect his fun and cheerful personality.

Earns: Business Ventures

Along with music producer and business partner Guy Oseary, Ashton Kutcher began two venture capital firms: Sound Ventures and A-Grade Ventures. After receiving the first round of funding worth $15 million, the partners invested in several businesses and turned it into $250 million, as noted by Forbes. After the second round of funding in 2018, Sound Ventures targeted $150 million for their third funding round as they had plans to invest in Bird. This transportation company manufactures electric scooters.

Spends: Real Estate

Ashton Kutcher and Mila Kunis love architecture. The couple has bought and sold several properties. Currently, the couple owns three properties. The first home they purchased together was a glamorous $12 million mansion in Beverly Hills. Some of their famous neighbors include Jennifer Lawrence, Katy Perry, and Adele. They bought a $10 million beach home in Santa Barbara that offers an outdoor barbecue during the lockdown. Lastly, the couple built a sustainable farmhouse in LA powered by photovoltaics, as reported by Architectural Digest.

Earns: Smart Investments

Known as the most active Silicon Valley investor in Hollywood, Ashton Kutcher has built a successful investing career. Through his ventures, the actor has invested in 61 companies until now. The most notable are: $1 million in Skype, $500,000 in Uber, $300,000 in Warby Parker, $2.5 million in Airbnb, $1.5 million in Houzz, $3 million in Spotify, and Bitcoin. Kutcher claimed research to be the most important part of investing, which led him to make smart investments in companies worth billions of dollars today.

Spends: Family Vacations

Ashton Kutcher has always loved going on vacations with his wife and family. The couple has been spotted on several romantic trips, including their 2010 trip to St. Barts and their 2012 vacation to Bali, Indonesia. Before the pandemic hit, they had a chance to take their children on a fun trip to Disneyland, where they discovered the rides and adventures for a weekend.

Earns: Renting Properties

When Ashton Kutcher could not sell his $12 million Beverly Hills home, he decided to turn it into a lucrative cash-earning opportunity. The couple set a price tag of $55,000 if the house is leased for a month. As the family spends their time between the Beach Home and Farmhouse, the idle home has become an earning source for Kutcher and the family.

Spends: Philanthropy

Along with making significant investments and taking selective roles in movies, Ashton Kutcher is also a real-time hero, saving thousands of lives through his non-profit organization. He founded Thorn: Digital Defenders of Children with his ex-wife Demi Moore and the organization has helped save over 6000 children from becoming sex-trafficking victims. According to Fortune, he also donated $4 million in cryptocurrency to the Ellen DeGeneres Wildlife Fund that supports the conservation of endangered species.

Ashton Kutcher has done it all. Long-running sitcoms, rom-com movies, quick-earning investments, and saved lives through his charity work. The actor likes to stay ahead of the trend and also spend his fortune on simple luxuries in life. Ashton Kutcher will next be seen starring in an NFT animated series called Stoner Cats and his wife, Mila Kunis.
