Biography

Robert Marawa (born March 1, 1973) is a prominent South African sports journalist, accomplished radio host, and seasoned television presenter. 

His profound knowledge and extensive expertise have established him as one of the most respected and knowledgeable figures in his field. Marawa has lent his talents to various high-profile sports programs, including the immensely popular Soccerzone, Extra Time, and Thursday Night Live, captivating audiences nationwide with his engaging commentary.

Beyond his contributions to broadcasting, Robert Marawa is highly sought after as a motivational speaker, wielding his words of wisdom and encouragement to inspire and empower diverse audiences.

Early Life & Education

Born on March 1, 1973, in Vryheid, KwaZulu-Natal, Robert Marawa is a renowned South African personality. He spent his formative years alongside his siblings, including notable sisters Gugu Marawa and Vanessa Marawa. 

Both of his sisters have significantly impacted South Africa, showcasing the influence of a talented and accomplished family. The proud parents of the Marawa siblings are Frank Marawa and Phumile Marawa, who played pivotal roles in nurturing their children’s talents and shaping their destinies.

Robert Marawa‘s educational journey commenced at Hilton College, an esteemed institution in Pietermaritzburg, KwaZulu-Natal. However, he decided to make a strategic shift and transferred to the University of Witwatersrand in 1992, where he embarked on a journey to pursue a bachelor’s degree in law.

Career

Robert Marawa embarked on his career journey in 1996, initially working as a continuity presenter on SABC1. As time progressed, his involvement in sports programming increased, leading him to host well-received shows such as Laduma and Soccerzone. Marawa‘s breakthrough came in 1998 when he was chosen as the primary presenter for SABC1’s coverage of the FIFA World Cup in France. This opportunity showcased his profound expertise and unwavering passion for sports journalism.

Beyond his television ventures, Marawa has carved a successful path in radio hosting. His notable shows include Metro FM’s 083 Sports@6 and Talk Radio 702’s Robert Marawa Sports Show. The combination of extensive experience and unwavering dedication has solidified Marawa‘s standing as one of the most respected figures in South African sports media.

In recognition of his contributions, Robert Marawa earned the esteemed title of South African Sports Journalist of the Year in 2013. Furthermore, his remarkable impact on arts, culture, literature, and journalism was honored with the Order of Ikhamanga in Silver. This accolade is the pinnacle of recognition in South Africa’s arts and culture sector, cementing Robert Marawa‘s status as a highly respected and accomplished professional.
