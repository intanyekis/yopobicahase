Wilmer Valderrama is an American actor, singer, television personality, and producer, best known for his role as Fez on the sitcom That ’70s Show and as Carlos Madrigal on an American horror television series called From Dusk Till Dawn. The actor is also credited for his work in Party Monster , Unaccompanied Minors , as well as Charming .

Early life and education of Wilmer Valderrama

Wilmer Valderrama was born in Miami, Florida, United States , on January 30, 1980 . He is the son of Sobeida and Balbino A. Valderrama who owns a farm equipment rental company. At the age of 3, he and his parents moved to Venezuela and returned to the United States again, along with his 3 siblings named Cristiano, Marilyn and Stephaine .

He attended William Howard Taft Charter High School . He is of mixed Colombian-Venezuelan ethnicity and is a US citizen.

Wilmer Valderrama’s professional career

Valderrama began his career by appearing in numerous plays, including A Midsummer Night’s Dream and Rumor . Later he made his first professional career in the Pacific Bell commercial in Spanish.

He later played Fez on That ’70s Show from 1998 to 2006 and was a senior in high school when the pilot episode was filmed. Valderrama also appeared as DJ Keoki in the 2003 movie called Party Freak teaming up with Macaulay Culkin, Chloe Sevigny and Seth Green .

In addition, he also produced and hosted the MTV series, a television show called Yo Momma in the year 2006 that lasted for one year. He also appeared in the Los Angeles Times Critic’s Choice. Blackout which is an adaptation of the Drunk feature films . On June 16, 2016 Valderrama was set to join NCIS for its fourteenth season and will be a series regular.

In addition to his acting career, Valderrama also has a musical career of his own. As of May 11, 2011, he released the song called ‘The Way I Fiesta’ in which he performed as Eduardo Fresco . Valderrama also appeared in and produced a famous 2011 music video called LMFAO’s ‘I’m Sexy and I Know it’.
